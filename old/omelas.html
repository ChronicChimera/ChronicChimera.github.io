Original Story

1. The Ones Who Walk Away From Omelas is a thought-provoking allegory that explores the implementation of utilitarianism and the morality of sacrificing the happiness of a few for the greater good of the many.

2. The story is set in the fictional city of Omelas, which is described as a utopia where the citizens live in abundance and enjoy a high standard of living.

3. Inexplicably, the city's prosperity and the happiness of its people is contingent on the suffering of a single child, who wastes away in a dark room, deprived of everything but basic nutrition.

4. This story raises the question of whether or not we're morally obligated to intervene when we witness injustice, even if it means sacrificing our own happiness.
